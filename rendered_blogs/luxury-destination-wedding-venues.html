Destinations

1. Château de Vaux-le-Vicomte, Maincy, France: A Royal Fairytale

The Pinnacle of French Grandeur

Located just an hour south of Paris, Château de Vaux-le-Vicomte is the masterpiece that inspired the Palace of Versailles. Built in the mid-17th century for Nicolas Fouquet, the Superintendent of Finances, it features jaw-dropping Baroque architecture and grand formal gardens designed by André Le Nôtre. Hosting a wedding here is the closest one can get to royalty. The estate is a symphony of fountains, gravel pathways, grand salons, and gilded ceilings, offering a setting of unparalleled elegance.

The Perfect Wedding Setup

Exchange vows on the grand steps of the château, looking out over the symmetrical lawns and water basins. For the reception, the **Grand Salon**—a circular hall with an soaring dome and marble pillars—hosts banquet dining beneath historic murals. The celebration culminate in the gardens with a private, orchestra-backed fireworks display reflecting off the canal. Exclusive buyouts for a weekend celebration start around $120,000 USD for venue rental alone, with full productions easily reaching $300,000+ USD.

Unmissable Couple Detail: Candlelit Magic

Recreate the historic candlelit evenings of the 17th century. The estate can be lit entirely by over 2,000 candles placed throughout the château gardens and windows, creating a soft, warm, romantic glow that feels completely disconnected from the modern world.

2. Amangiri, Utah, USA: Modernist Sanctuary in the Canyon

Dramatic Geometry and Desert Intimacy

For couples who prefer clean lines, raw nature, and architectural drama, Amangiri is the ultimate sanctuary. Located in southern Utah, the resort is tucked into a protected valley near the Grand Staircase-Escalante National Monument. The modernist concrete structure blends seamlessly into the red rock canyons, offering a serene, meditative backdrop that celebrates the harsh beauty of the American Southwest.

The Perfect Wedding Setup

Say your vows on the **Desert Lounge**, an open-air pavilion built against a sheer, 165-million-year-old sandstone rock face, offering views of the vast desert mesas. The reception is held around the iconic swimming pool, which curves around a massive limestone rock spur under the desert stars. Due to its exclusivity, weddings at Amangiri generally require a full resort buyout, starting at $150,000 to $250,000 USD depending on the season.

Unmissable Couple Detail: Desert Stargazing Toast

As the desert air cools, host a stargazing cocktail reception. With no light pollution for miles, the Milky Way becomes your ceiling, allowing you and your guests to toast with local mezcal cocktails around open fire pits.

3. Nihi Sumba, Indonesia: Wild Luxury & Ocean Romance

The Edge of Wildness

Located on the remote island of Sumba, a 50-minute flight from Bali, Nihi Sumba is a resort that redefines barefoot luxury. Known for its world-class surf wave ('Occy’s Left'), private villas with thatched roofs, and the Nihiwatu beach, it is a venue that celebrates wild, untamed nature. A wedding here is perfect for couples who want the exotic beauty of Indonesia but seek a raw, spiritual connection far removed from Bali's commercial crowds.

The Perfect Wedding Setup

The **Ruha Wave Pavilion**, perched on a cliff overlooking the pounding surf of the Indian Ocean, offers a dramatic setting for sunset vows. Alternatively, marry on the 2.5-kilometer private beach, flanked by Sumbanese horses roaming the shoreline. For the reception, host a barefoot feast on the sand under a canopy of fairy lights, featuring fresh coconut-husks barbecued lobster. Wedding buyouts and custom packages average $40,000 to $100,000 USD.

Unmissable Couple Detail: Pre-Wedding Spa Safari

Treat your bridal party to the **Nihi Oka Spa Safari**. Guests take a scenic morning trek through rice paddies and cliffside villages to a private valley on the ocean. Spend the day enjoying unlimited spa treatments in open-air clifftop pavilions, dining on fresh organic meals, and swimming in a private cove.

Practicalities

Practicalities for Couples

    Guest Travel Logistics

    Remote venues like Amangiri and Nihi Sumba require careful travel planning. Arrange private shuttle services from nearest airports (Page Municipal Airport for Amangiri, Tambolaka Airport for Nihi Sumba) and provide a detailed travel concierge service for guests.

    Backup Weather Plans

    For outdoor-focused venues like Amangiri and Vaux-le-Vicomte, ensure the venue has adequate indoor spaces that match your aesthetic in case of unexpected rain or sandstorms.

    Seasonality

    Desert heat at Amangiri can be extreme in July/August, while France experiences cold winters. Book during the shoulder seasons (spring/autumn) for optimal climate comfort.
