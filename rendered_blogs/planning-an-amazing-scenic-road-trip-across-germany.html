The Romantic Republic: Germany Beyond the Tourist Trail

Germany is one of the most underrated romantic destinations in Europe. While it rarely tops honeymoon lists, the country possesses a landscape of extraordinary diversity—medieval walled towns, baroque palace gardens, fairy-tale castles looming over river gorges, dense primordial forest, and glittering alpine lakes. For couples who love the open road, a German scenic road trip is one of the most rewarding and visually spectacular journeys in all of Europe.

The key to unlocking Germany's romance is to leave the motorways behind and follow the Bundesstraßen—the scenic two-lane regional roads that thread through vineyard valleys, past half-timbered village squares, and along the shores of mountain lakes. Germany's famous Romantische Straße (Romantic Road) is the most famous route, but we have extended the journey to include the Rhine Valley, the Black Forest, and the Bavarian lakes district for the ultimate romantic itinerary.

Strategic Context

Strategic Context: Timing & Route

The Best Time for a German Road Trip

Late May through June and September through October are the absolute best windows. In late May, the Bavarian Alps are snow-free and green, the Rhine Valley vineyards are in leaf, and the Rhine Gorge castles are accessible without summer crowds. September brings the harvest season in the wine regions, the beginning of oktoberfest (if that is of interest), and the early autumn foliage on the Black Forest heights. Avoid mid-July to mid-August—peak summer crowds, especially around Neuschwanstein Castle, are intense.

The Recommended Route (10 to 14 Days)

  1. Frankfurt (1 night): Arrive, explore the restored Römerberg medieval quarter, and pick up your rental car.
  2. Rhine Gorge & Moselle Valley (2 nights): Drive the Rhine Valley road through the legendary Lorelei gorge, past the Burg Rheinfels and Burg Gutenfels castles, then divert to the Moselle for German Riesling tasting.
  3. Romantic Road: Würzburg to Dinkelsbühl (2 nights): Follow the Baroque Würzburg Residenz palace gardens through the medieval walled town of Rothenburg ob der Tauber (one of Europe's best-preserved medieval towns) to the perfectly intact fortified town of Dinkelsbühl.
  4. Neuschwanstein Castle & Füssen (2 nights): The fairytale castle par excellence—the inspiration for Disney's Sleeping Beauty castle. Stay in Füssen for the Alpsee lake reflections.
  5. Bavaria's Alpine Lakes (2 nights): Drive the German Alpine Road east through Garmisch-Partenkirchen, pausing at the Zugspitze (Germany's highest peak) and the ethereally beautiful Eibsee lake.
  6. Black Forest (2 nights): Loop west through the dense forests and spa towns of Baden-Baden, ending at the Titisee-Neustadt lake for a final night at a historic Black Forest hotel.
Destinations

The Unmissable Romantic Stops

Burg Eltz, Moselle Valley

The single most romantic castle in Germany—and an argument could be made: in all of Europe. Unlike Neuschwanstein's theatrical Disney-pastiche, Burg Eltz is the real deal: a medieval fortress perched on a forested rock spur above the Moselle Valley, rising eight stories in a cluster of pointed turrets, and owned by the same family for 33 continuous generations. Approach on foot through the beech forest; the moment the castle appears through the trees is genuinely breathtaking. Arrive at 9 AM for the opening tour before the coaches arrive.

Rothenburg ob der Tauber

The best-preserved medieval walled town in Germany—and in Europe, arguably. Walking the full circuit of the town walls at dusk, with the half-timbered houses and Gothic church spires silhouetted against the evening sky, is as close to actual time travel as a couple can get. Spend the night to experience the town after the day-trippers leave; after 7 PM, Rothenburg belongs entirely to you.

Eibsee Lake, Bavaria

One of Germany's most perfectly beautiful lakes—a high-altitude glacial lake at the foot of the Zugspitze, its water a supernatural shade of turquoise-green. Rent a rowing boat and drift around the lake's wooded islands in the morning silence, with the Zugspitze's snow-dusted summit reflected in the water.

Accommodations

Where to Stay: Romantic German Accommodation

    Hotel Burghotel Auf Schönburg (Rhine Valley)

    A genuine medieval castle hotel perched on a rocky spur directly above the Rhine, offering jaw-dropping river gorge views from every room. Rates: €150 to €350 per night.

    Romantik Hotel Markusturm (Rothenburg)

    A carefully restored 13th-century inn within the old town walls—think stone vaulted ceilings, four-poster beds, and a candlelit breakfast in a medieval dining room. Rates: €130 to €250 per night.

    Bareiss Resort (Black Forest)

    One of Germany's finest luxury hotels—a Michelin-starred resort in the deep Black Forest village of Baiersbronn, featuring a full spa, indoor/outdoor pools, and extraordinary regional cuisine. Rates: €400 to €800 per night.

Photography

The Photography Spot Guide

    Burg Eltz from the Moselle Footpath

    Hike the 1.5km woodland path from the car park to the castle. The best photograph is shot from the path just as the castle emerges from the beech forest, using the trees as natural frames. Optimal time: Morning golden hour.

    Rothenburg ob der Tauber: The Plönlein

    The most photographed corner in Germany—a medieval half-timbered corner house at a split junction of two cobblestone lanes, framed by a circular tower. Best shot in the early morning with empty streets.

    Eibsee Lake at Dawn

    Arrive before sunrise and capture the Zugspitze summit perfectly mirrored in the glassy lake surface. The turquoise-green hue of the water in early morning light is extraordinary.

Practicalities

Practicalities for Couples

    Car Rental

    Pick up and return at Frankfurt Airport (FRA). Rent an automatic transmission vehicle if you are not familiar with manual driving on the Autobahn. GPS is essential.

    Autobahn Etiquette

    The left lane on the Autobahn is strictly for overtaking. Keep right at all times unless overtaking. Speed is unrestricted on large sections—drive to your comfort level.

    Fuel

    Germany uses unleaded petrol (Super). Premium diesel (Diesel) is available for diesel vehicles. Fuel costs are roughly equivalent to UK prices.

    Currency

    Euro (€). Credit cards are increasingly accepted, but carry cash for smaller villages and castle admission fees.
