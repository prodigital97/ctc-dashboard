# Audit 1: Historic Cafés of Europe (ID: 353)

### 🚨 Critical Discrepancies & Findings (Verified June 2026)

1. **Café Central (Vienna) - Temporary Closure / Renovations**:
   - **Live Search Fact**: Café Central in Vienna closed on **March 16, 2026**, for comprehensive renovations and modernization in celebration of its 150th anniversary. It is expected to **reopen in the autumn of 2026**.
   - **Correction**: Recommend calling out this closure to readers so couples do not plan a date only to find a construction site. Direct them to **DECENTRAL**, their nearby pop-up café in the Palais Harrach on the Freyung, which serves the original patisserie and coffee specialties.
2. **Caffè Florian (Venice) - Hidden Surcharges**:
   - **Live Search Fact**: Guests sitting at outdoor tables during live performances of the terrace orchestra are charged a supplemental **€6 per person** music fee. Drinks and food prices are also significantly higher for outdoor seating than indoor.
   - **Correction**: Add a "Couple's Friction Alert" warning readers about the €6 music surcharge so they can choose to sit inside or stand nearby in the piazza to enjoy the music for free.
3. **Café de Flore (Paris) - Practical Friction & Pricing**:
   - **Live Search Fact**: Café de Flore does not accept reservations. Wait times at peak hours (mid-day and weekends) frequently exceed 30 minutes. Hot chocolate is €9.50 and club sandwiches are €22.50.
   - **Correction**: Provide specific advice to visit during "fringe hours" (early morning between 7:30 AM – 9:00 AM or late evening after 9:00 PM) for an intimate setting, and warn about the premium pricing.
4. **Hotel Pricing Updates**:
   - **Hotel Sacher (Vienna)**: Room rates start closer to €550–€700+ for standard/deluxe rooms (though suites like the Opera View Suite can exceed $1,200). Adjust the range to reflect standard luxury entry rates: **$650 - $1,300 USD per night**.
   - **Sina Centurion Palace (Venice)**: Entry-level rooms start around **$470 - $950 USD per night** depending on the season, with Grand Canal suites higher.

### 📐 Structural & Brand Violations
- **Generic Headings**: The original post used block names directly: `<h2>Strategic Context: Cozy Afternoon Stops</h2>`, `<h2>Accommodations: Grand European Palace Hotels</h2>`, `<h2>Handpicked Couple Experiences</h2>`, and `<h2>The Photography Spot Guide</h2>`.
- **Correction**: Replace them with organic, Conde-Nast-style editorial headings that flow naturally (e.g. "Planning the Escape: Cozy Afternoon Stops", "Gilded Dreams: Grand European Palace Hotels", and "Capturing the Glow: A Couple's Photography Guide").
